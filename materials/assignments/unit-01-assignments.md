# Unit 1 — Assignments: Introduction to Basic Computing

**Course:** Computer Information | **Hours:** 6 | **Objectives:** Obj 1, 2, 9

## A1.1 — Computing Foundations Short Portfolio
- **Type:** individual
- **Time:** 3–4 hours
- **Objectives:** Obj 1 [Understand], Obj 2 [Understand], Obj 9 [Apply]

### Student prompt
1. Draw/label the IPO + storage model and explain each part with a legal-work example (1 page).
2. Create a professional folder structure for three fictional clients and document naming rules (½ page).
3. Write a 300-word note: “Applications of AI in the legal profession” (survey level; cite any class sources used).
4. Submit a screenshot log of: creating folders, renaming a PDF judgment, and sending a professional practice email to your instructor (or draft email text if screenshots restricted).

### Rubric (25 marks)
| Criterion | Excellent | Adequate | Needs work |
|---|---|---|---|
| IPO accuracy | Clear + legal examples | Mostly correct | Confused parts |
| File discipline | Clear structure + naming | Partial | Unusable |
| AI short note | Accurate survey | Thin | Incorrect |
| Practical evidence | Complete | Partial | Missing |
| Presentation | Neat, labeled | Readable | Careless |

## A1.2 — Digital Communication Mini-Brief
Draft a one-page professional email policy for a student legal clinic (subject lines, attachments, confidentiality, reply expectations).
