## Assignment 1 — Digital Chambers Starter Kit (Individual)

**Marks suggestion:** 10  
**Submission:** PDF or DOCX  

### Task
Create a one-page “Digital Chambers Protocol” for a junior intern including:

1. Folder structure for one client matter (at least 5 folders).  
2. File-naming rules (with 3 good examples and 3 bad examples).  
3. Daily backup rule (what + when + where).  
4. Screen-lock / password hygiene rules.  
5. One paragraph on why these habits protect client confidentiality.

### Evaluation focus
Clarity, practicality, professional tone, and Unit-1 concept application.

---

## Assignment 2 — Internet Research Mini Lab (Individual)

**Marks suggestion:** 10  

### Task
Using only authentic/official or clearly identified sources:

1. Find the official webpage of any one Indian court (Supreme Court / any High Court).  
2. Note the URL and what information is available (cause list / judgments / circulars).  
3. Write 150–200 words on how a BA-LLB student should evaluate whether an online legal source is reliable.  
4. Attach screenshots (optional) with date of access.

### Caution
Do not scrape or download copyrighted databases unlawfully. Use publicly accessible official pages.

## Assignment 3 — Digital Communication Role-Play (Pair Work)

**Marks suggestion:** 10  

### Scenario
A client asks by WhatsApp: “Can we win this case tomorrow? Reply fast.”

### Task
1. Student A writes an **unprofessional** reply (what not to do).  
2. Student B writes a **professional** reply (tone, caution, next steps, no overpromise).  
3. Together, submit a 1-page reflection:
   - Risks of casual legal promises on chat  
   - When to shift from WhatsApp to email  
   - What records should be kept

## Assignment 4 — AI Assisted, Human Verified (Individual)

**Marks suggestion:** 15  

### Task
1. Choose any one Unit-1 topic (e.g., OS / Internet / AI in law).  
2. Ask any AI tool to generate a 150-word explanation.  
3. Paste the AI output.  
4. Rewrite a corrected student version.  
5. Add a table:

| AI Claim / Sentence | Verified? (Yes/No/Partial) | Your Correction / Comment |
|---|---|---|
|  |  |  |

6. Final reflection (100 words): benefits + risks (hallucination, bias, confidentiality).

### Learning outcome
Students practice Unit-5 ethics early: AI is assistant, not authority.
