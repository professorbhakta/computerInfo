## Section A — Easy [Easy]

### E1
**[Easy] [Remember] [Obj 1]** Define a computer in one sentence suitable for a legal practice context.

**Answer:** A computer is an electronic device that accepts data as input, processes it according to programmed instructions, and produces information as output for tasks such as drafting, research, and record-keeping.

---

### E2
**[Easy] [Remember] [Obj 1]** Name the four functional units of a computer system in the IPO model.

**Answer:** Input, Process, Output, and Storage.

---

### E3
**[Easy] [Remember] [Obj 1]** What is the primary function of an operating system?

**Answer:** An operating system manages hardware resources, provides a user interface, and runs application software (e.g., Word, browser) on behalf of the user.

---

### E4
**[Easy] [Understand] [Obj 1]** Distinguish between hardware and software with one example of each used in a law office.

**Answer:** Hardware is physical equipment (e.g., printer, scanner); software is programs/instructions (e.g., MS Word, case-management application).

---

### E5
**[Easy] [Remember] [Obj 2]** What does "AI" stand for, and give one legal application.

**Answer:** Artificial Intelligence. Example application: automated legal research, document summarization, or predictive case analytics.

---

### E6
**[Easy] [Remember] [Obj 3]** What is the purpose of "Track Changes" in MS Word?

**Answer:** It records insertions, deletions, and formatting edits so reviewers can see who changed what and accept or reject revisions—essential for collaborative legal drafting.

---

### E7
**[Easy] [Remember] [Obj 5]** What is the difference between an Excel workbook and a worksheet?

**Answer:** A workbook is the entire Excel file (.xlsx); a worksheet is a single tab/sheet within that workbook.

---

### E8
**[Easy] [Remember] [Obj 5]** Write the Excel formula to sum values in cells B2 through B10.

**Answer:** `=SUM(B2:B10)`

---

### E9
**[Easy] [Remember] [Obj 6]** State two design principles for effective legal PowerPoint slides.

**Answer:** Any two of: one main idea per slide; readable font size; high contrast; minimal text; consistent theme; purposeful (not excessive) animations.

---

### E10
**[Easy] [Understand] [Obj 8]** Define "hallucination" in the context of generative AI used for legal work.

**Answer:** A hallucination is when an AI system confidently produces incorrect or fabricated information—such as fake case citations or non-existent statutes.

---

### E11
**[Easy] [Remember] [Obj 4]** List three standard sections of a legal notice.

**Answer:** Heading (parties and date); body (facts, legal basis, demand/relief); signature block with advocate details and place.

---

### E12
**[Easy] [Understand] [Obj 1]** What is digital communication? Give two examples relevant to legal practice.

**Answer:** Exchange of information through electronic channels. Examples: professional email, video conferencing for client meetings, secure messaging within a firm.

## Section B — Medium [Medium]

### M1
**[Medium] [Understand] [Obj 1]** Explain the roles of input, process, output, and storage when a law student searches a judgment database and saves a PDF.

**Answer:** Input: keyboard/mouse queries and clicks. Process: OS and browser run the search; server retrieves results. Output: results displayed on screen; PDF rendered. Storage: judgment saved to disk/cloud; browser cache may temporarily hold data.

---

### M2
**[Medium] [Apply] [Obj 3]** Outline the steps to create a mail merge for sending individualized hearing notices to 50 clients from an Excel list.

**Answer:** (1) Prepare Excel data source with fields (name, case no., date). (2) Create Word notice template with merge fields. (3) Mailings → Select Recipients → Use Existing List. (4) Insert merge fields. (5) Preview results. (6) Finish & Merge → Edit Individual Documents or Print.

---

### M3
**[Medium] [Apply] [Obj 4]** Describe the structural elements of an affidavit suitable for filing.

**Answer:** Title/caption; introductory paragraph (deponent identity, competence); numbered paragraphs of facts in first person; verification clause ("verified at… on this…"); signature of deponent; attestation by oath commissioner/notary with seal.

---

### M4
**[Medium] [Apply] [Obj 5]** A case tracker has columns: Case ID (A), Status (B), Hearing Date (C). Write a formula in D2 to display "Urgent" if hearing is within 7 days of TODAY(), else "Routine". (Assume C2 holds a valid date.)

**Answer:** `=IF(C2-TODAY()<=7,"Urgent","Routine")`  
(Alternative acceptable: `=IF(C2<=TODAY()+7,"Urgent","Routine")`)

---

### M5
**[Medium] [Apply] [Obj 5]** Explain how AutoFilter helps manage a chambers case register with 200 matters.

**Answer:** AutoFilter displays only rows matching criteria (e.g., status = "Pending", court = "High Court") without deleting data, enabling quick review of subsets, hearing lists, and advocate workload—critical for daily case management.

---

### M6
**[Medium] [Apply] [Obj 5]** A billing sheet lists hours in column E and rate in column F. What formula in G2 calculates fee for row 2?

**Answer:** `=E2*F2`

---

### M7
**[Medium] [Apply] [Obj 6]** Sketch the recommended slide sequence for a moot court presentation (list slide titles only).

**Answer:** Title → Facts → Issues Presented → Arguments for Petitioner → Arguments for Respondent (or rebuttal) → Authorities Cited → Prayer/Relief Sought → Thank You / Questions.

---

### M8
**[Medium] [Understand] [Obj 2]** Briefly compare rule-based AI and machine-learning AI with one legal example each.

**Answer:** Rule-based systems follow explicit if-then logic (e.g., tax calculator from fixed rules). Machine-learning systems learn patterns from data (e.g., predicting case outcomes from historical judgments). Both may appear in legal tech with different transparency implications.

---

### M9
**[Medium] [Analyze] [Obj 8]** A junior associate pastes a client's full case file into a public generative AI chatbot to draft a petition. Identify three ethical/legal risks.

**Answer:** (1) Breach of client confidentiality and professional duty. (2) Potential violation of data-protection obligations. (3) Unreliable output (hallucinated citations) creating malpractice risk; possible unauthorized practice concerns if used without supervision.

---

### M10
**[Medium] [Apply] [Obj 3]** How do headers, footers, and page numbers support court filing requirements?

**Answer:** They provide consistent identification (case title, document type), pagination for multi-page filings, and professional layout meeting chamber/court conventions—reducing rejection for formatting defects.

---

### M11
**[Medium] [Understand] [Obj 7]** What is "human-in-the-loop" review when using AI for legal summarization?

**Answer:** A qualified person reads the source material and the AI summary, verifies accuracy against originals, corrects errors, and takes professional responsibility before the summary is used in advice or filings.

---

### M12
**[Medium] [Apply] [Obj 9]** List four file-management practices for secure legal work on a shared lab computer.

**Answer:** Log out after use; save to designated user folder not desktop; use meaningful file names (client matter + doc type + date); avoid leaving confidential PDFs in downloads; back up to approved cloud/USB per institutional policy.

## Section C — Hard [Hard]

### H1
**[Hard] [Analyze] [Obj 1, 9]** A firm's internet fails during an online hearing. Map which computer components and services are involved and suggest a contingency using digital communication tools already covered in the syllabus.

**Answer:** Components: NIC/router (hardware), OS network stack, browser/VC app (software), ISP (external). Contingency: switch to mobile hotspot (alternate network), notify court via official email/phone, join via smartphone app, ensure backup of documents on local storage; document the disruption for the record.

---

### H2
**[Hard] [Apply] [Obj 4, 3]** Draft an outline (headings and bullet points only) for a rent agreement between landlord and tenant using Word structural conventions—not full legal text.

**Answer:** Title; Parties clause; Recitals; Definitions; Term and rent; Security deposit; Maintenance and utilities; Termination; Dispute resolution; Governing law; Witness block; Schedule (property description); Signature lines. Formatting: styles for headings, numbered clauses, page numbers in footer, table for rent schedule optional.

---

### H3
**[Hard] [Apply] [Obj 5]** Given a client table (A: Client ID, B: Name, C: Matter Type, D: Fees Due), explain how you would use SORT, FILTER, and a chart to prepare a monthly collections report for the senior partner.

**Answer:** FILTER rows where Fees Due > 0; SORT by Fees Due descending; add SUM for total outstanding; insert column chart by Matter Type or top 10 clients by amount; label chart with date range; ensure client identifiers are handled per privacy policy before sharing.

---

### H4
**[Hard] [Analyze] [Obj 5]** Why does merging cells in a case-tracking spreadsheet often break sorting and filtering? How would you redesign the sheet?

**Answer:** Merged cells create irregular ranges that Excel treats as single blocks, disrupting column-wise sort/filter logic. Redesign: one row per case; use "Wrap Text" and consistent column widths instead of merges; use Center Across Selection for titles if needed; keep header row unmerged.

---

### H5
**[Hard] [Create] [Obj 6]** Design specifications for a 10-slide "Legal Awareness: Cybercrime" presentation including where to use SmartArt, a chart, and one animation—and justify each choice.

**Answer:** e.g., Slide 3 SmartArt (process: report → investigate → prosecute) for clarity; Slide 7 chart (bar: complaint categories) for data impact; Slide 5 single animation (bullet reveal) to pace definitions; avoid animating every slide; speaker notes for IPC/IT Act references; high-contrast theme for classroom projection.

---

### H6
**[Hard] [Evaluate] [Obj 8, 7]** An AI legal research tool returns a citation that does not appear in the official reporter. Construct a step-by-step verification protocol before citing it in a memo.

**Answer:** (1) Search citation in authoritative database (SCC Online, Manupatra, official court site). (2) Read the actual judgment heading and headnote. (3) Confirm jurisdiction and year match the proposition. (4) Cross-check parallel citations. (5) If unverifiable, discard and note AI error. (6) Document human verification in work product; do not rely on AI alone.

---

### H7
**[Hard] [Evaluate] [Obj 8]** Compare responsible and irresponsible use of generative AI in drafting a bail application across dimensions: accuracy, privacy, bias, and accountability.

**Answer:** Responsible: redacted facts, verified citations, senior review, disclosure if required by court rules, jurisdiction-specific arguments. Irresponsible: confidential data uploaded, uncited AI text filed as-is, generic arguments ignoring accused's socio-economic context (bias), no advocate sign-off. Accountability rests with the advocate, not the tool.

---

### H8
**[Hard] [Analyze] [Obj 2, 8]** Discuss three limitations of AI in law that remain relevant even as models improve, with syllabus-aligned examples.

**Answer:** (1) Hallucination/false citations—undermines research integrity. (2) Jurisdictional nuance—training data may not reflect local statute/court hierarchy. (3) Ethical accountability—AI cannot exercise professional judgment, client counseling, or appear in court; bias in training data may skew outcomes in sentencing analytics or contract review.
