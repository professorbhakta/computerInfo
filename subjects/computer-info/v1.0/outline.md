# Computer Information: Full Syllabus (Units 1–5)

## Metadata
- **Subject:** Computer Information
- **Slug:** computer-info
- **Version:** v1.0
- **Audience:** Law students (BA-LLB / equivalent)
- **Level:** Introductory applied computing for legal practice
- **Exam focus:** SEE + CIA (assignments, seminars, presentations, MCQs, practical)
- **Duration / timeline:** 30 teaching hours mapped (5 × 6 hrs) + weekly lab
- **Credits:** 2 (2 lecture + 1 lab hrs/week; total 50 marks)
- **Prerequisites:** Knowledge of basic computing
- **Rationale:** Develop essential computer and AI skills for legal documentation, research, data management, and professional presentations; promote ethical AI use in legal practice.

## Learning Objectives

Mapped to syllabus Course Outcomes (COs).

1. **[Understand]** Explain the definition of a computer, system components, operating systems, internet basics, and digital communication in everyday legal work.  
2. **[Understand]** Summarize the history of AI, core technique families at a conceptual level, applications in the legal profession, and future scope.  
3. **[Apply]** Create and format professional legal documents in MS Word, including page layout, headers/footers, tables, mail merge, references/citations, and track changes.  
4. **[Apply]** Draft notices, agreements, petitions, and affidavits using MS Word conventions appropriate to student legal practice.  
5. **[Apply]** Build and manage legal data in MS Excel (workbooks, entry/formatting, formulas/functions, sort/filter, charts) for case tracking, client records, and billing.  
6. **[Create]** Design moot court, case, and seminar presentations in MS PowerPoint using themes/templates, SmartArt/charts, and purposeful animations/transitions.  
7. **[Apply]** Use AI tools for legal research, summarization, and assisted drafting with clear human oversight.  
8. **[Evaluate]** Judge AI outputs for privacy, bias, confidentiality, accuracy, and professional responsibility; state limitations of AI in law.  
9. **[Apply]** Demonstrate syllabus practical skills across file management, Word drafting, Excel sheets, PowerPoint presentations, and ethical AI comparison tasks.

## Topic Sequence

### T1 — Introduction to Basic Computing
- **Duration:** 6 hours | **Weight:** 10%
- **Objectives covered:** 1, 2 (intro portion), 9
- **Key concepts:** Computer definition; hardware/software/peopleware; input–process–output–storage; OS functions; internet & browsers; email/cloud/digital communication; AI definition, brief history, legal applications, technique overview, future scope
- **Teaching notes:** Keep hardware brief; emphasize legal workflows (research databases, secure file handling, professional email). Introduce AI here at survey level; Unit 5 deepens ethics.
- **Exam tips:** Definitions + component identification + OS purpose + “AI in legal profession” short notes are high-yield.
- **Common pitfalls:** Confusing system software vs application software; treating AI as only ChatGPT.
- **Evidence of learning:** Label a system diagram; explain a legal research session using OS + browser + database.

### T2 — MS Word for Legal Documentation
- **Duration:** 6 hours | **Weight:** 10%
- **Objectives covered:** 3, 4, 9
- **Key concepts:** Create/format docs; styles; page layout; headers/footers; tables; mail merge; references/citations; track changes & comments; drafting notices, agreements, petitions, affidavits
- **Teaching notes:** Live-demo drafting; insist on styles over manual formatting; show track changes for senior review workflow.
- **Exam tips:** Steps of mail merge; purpose of track changes; structure of notice/affidavit.
- **Common pitfalls:** Spaces instead of tabs/styles; forgetting page numbers/margins for filing norms.
- **Evidence of learning:** Submit a formatted notice + tracked revision sample.

### T3 — MS Excel for Legal Data Management
- **Duration:** 6 hours | **Weight:** 10%
- **Objectives covered:** 5, 9
- **Key concepts:** Workbook/worksheet; data entry & formatting; formulas/functions (SUM, AVERAGE, IF, COUNTIF, VLOOKUP/XLOOKUP intro); sorting/filtering; charts; case tracking; client data; billing sheets
- **Teaching notes:** Use a realistic “chambers case register” dataset; privacy of client data while demoing.
- **Exam tips:** Difference workbook vs worksheet; write formulas; interpret a filter/chart for case status.
- **Common pitfalls:** Typing values instead of formulas; merging cells that break sorting.
- **Evidence of learning:** Case tracker + billing sheet with formulas and a chart.

### T4 — MS PowerPoint for Legal & Academic Presentations
- **Duration:** 6 hours | **Weight:** 10%
- **Objectives covered:** 6, 9
- **Key concepts:** Creating decks; slide design; themes/templates; animations/transitions; SmartArt/charts; legal case presentations; moot court & seminar decks
- **Teaching notes:** Advocacy of clarity over decoration; one idea per slide; speaker notes for moot.
- **Exam tips:** Design principles; when to use SmartArt vs bullets; moot deck structure (facts–issues–arguments–prayer).
- **Common pitfalls:** Wall-of-text slides; excessive animations; unreadable contrast.
- **Evidence of learning:** 8–12 slide moot or legal-awareness deck.

### T5 — AI and Ethical Use of AI in the Legal Profession
- **Duration:** 6 hours | **Weight:** 10%
- **Objectives covered:** 2, 7, 8, 9
- **Key concepts:** AI tools for legal research; generative AI basics; judiciary & legal analytics; AI-assisted drafting; ethics; privacy & data protection; bias; responsible use; limitations of AI in law
- **Teaching notes:** Pair tool demos with ethics critique; never paste confidential client data into public AI tools.
- **Exam tips:** Ethics short notes (bias, privacy, accountability); human-in-the-loop; limitations (hallucination, jurisdiction).
- **Common pitfalls:** Blind trust in AI citations; assuming AI “practices law.”
- **Evidence of learning:** Ethical analysis of AI-generated draft + human vs AI comparative memo.

## Glossary Seeds
| Term | Working definition |
|---|---|
| Computer | Electronic device that processes data into information under instructions |
| Operating System | System software managing hardware and providing services to applications |
| Mail Merge | Word feature producing personalized documents from a data source |
| Track Changes | Word revision mode showing insertions/deletions/comments for review |
| Workbook | Excel file containing one or more worksheets |
| Generative AI | AI that creates new text/images/etc. from patterns learned in training data |
| Hallucination | Confident but incorrect AI output (e.g., fake citations) |
| Bias (AI) | Systematic unfairness in AI outputs reflecting data or design skew |
| Human-in-the-loop | Practice of keeping accountable human review over AI outputs |
